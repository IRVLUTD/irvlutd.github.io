Abstract

We introduce a novel system for human-to-robot trajectory transfer that enables robots to manipulate objects by learning from human demonstration videos. The system consists of three modules. The first module is a video under- standing module that detects objects and extracts human hand trajectories from demonstration videos. The second module transfers a human-hand trajectory into a reference trajectory of a robot end-effector in the task space. The last module utilizes a trajectory optimization algorithm to solve a trajectory in the configuration space that can follow the end-effector trajectory transferred from the human demonstration. Con- sequently, these modules enable a robot to watch a human demonstration and then repeat the same manipulation task as in the demonstration, even when objects are placed differently from the demonstration. Experiments of different manipulation tasks are conducted on a mobile manipulator to verify the effectiveness of our system.
